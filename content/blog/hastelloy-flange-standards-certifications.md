# Hastelloy Flange Standards and Certifications Guide

When specifying **Hastelloy flanges** for critical applications, understanding applicable standards and required certifications is essential for ensuring quality, compliance, and long-term performance. This guide demystifies the alphabet soup of industry standards.

## Primary Standards for Hastelloy Flanges

### ASME B16.5: Pipe Flanges and Flanged Fittings

**The fundamental standard** for flange dimensions.

**Scope:**
- NPS 1/2" through 24"
- Pressure classes: 150, 300, 400, 600, 900, 1500, 2500
- All flange types and facings
- Bolt sizes and hole dimensions

**What it specifies:**
- Dimensional requirements
- Tolerances
- Pressure-temperature ratings
- Bolt hole arrangements
- Facing types and finishes
- Marking requirements

**Hastelloy-specific considerations:**
- Pressure-temperature ratings adjust for material strength
- Hastelloy's higher strength sometimes allows lower class
- Corrosion allowance typically not needed

### ASME B16.47: Large Diameter Steel Flanges

**For sizes NPS 26" through 60".**

**Two series:**

**Series A:**
- Based on B16.5 proportions
- More common internationally
- Heavier design

**Series B:**
- Different bolt circle diameter
- More widely stocked in North America
- Slightly lighter

**Important:** Series A and B are NOT interchangeable.

### ASME B16.48: Line Blanks

**For blind flanges used as isolation devices:**
- Spectacle blinds
- Spacers and spades
- Figure-8 blanks

**Hastelloy advantage:** Long service life means fewer removal cycles.

### ASTM B564: Nickel Alloy Forgings

**Material specification for Hastelloy flanges.**

**What it covers:**
- Chemical composition requirements
- Mechanical property requirements
- Heat treatment
- Testing and inspection
- Product marking

**Relevant alloy grades:**
- UNS N10276 (Hastelloy C276)
- UNS N06022 (Hastelloy C22)
- UNS N10665 (Hastelloy B-3)
- UNS N06002 (Hastelloy X)

**Key requirements:**
- Chemical analysis
- Tensile testing
- Hardness testing
- Grain size examination (when specified)

### NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156

**Critical for sour service (H2S environments).**

**Scope:**
- Oil and gas industry
- Environments containing H2S
- Sulfide stress cracking (SSC) prevention

**Hastelloy compliance:**
- C276: Compliant without restriction
- C22: Compliant without restriction
- No hardness limitations (unlike some steels)

**Requirements:**
- Material selection guidelines
- Hardness limits (not restrictive for Hastelloy)
- Testing and qualification procedures
- Documentation requirements

### PED 2014/68/EU: Pressure Equipment Directive

**European Union requirement.**

**Categories based on:**
- Pressure level
- Volume or diameter
- Fluid type (Group 1 or 2)

**Documentation:**
- Declaration of conformity
- Technical file
- CE marking
- Traceability

**Impact on Hastelloy flanges:**
- Material traceability critical
- Additional testing often required
- Authorized inspectors may be needed
- Comprehensive documentation essential

## Material Certifications

### Material Test Report (MTR)

**Essential documentation for every Hastelloy flange.**

**Type 3.1 Certificate per EN 10204:**

**Must include:**
- Manufacturer identification
- Product description
- Purchase order reference
- Heat number and unique identification
- Chemical composition (actual analysis)
- Mechanical properties (actual test results)
- Heat treatment details
- Test date and authorized signature

**Chemical composition must meet ASTM B564:**

**Hastelloy C276 example:**
- Nickel: 57% minimum
- Molybdenum: 15-17%
- Chromium: 14.5-16.5%
- Iron: 4-7%
- Tungsten: 3-4.5%
- Cobalt: 2.5% maximum
- Carbon: 0.01% maximum (typical)
- Silicon, Manganese, others per specification

**Mechanical properties:**
- Tensile strength: ≥100 ksi (690 MPa)
- Yield strength: ≥41 ksi (283 MPa)
- Elongation: ≥40%

### Additional Testing and Certification

**PMI (Positive Material Identification):**
- Handheld XRF analysis
- Verifies alloy composition
- Non-destructive
- Quick verification
- Cost: $50-100 per piece

**Dimensional Inspection Report:**
- Verifies ASME B16.5 compliance
- Critical dimensions measured
- Tolerance verification
- First Article Inspection (FAI) for large orders

**Hydrostatic Testing:**
- Not always required for flanges
- Sometimes specified for critical service
- Typically 1.5× design pressure
- Documentation provided

**Radiographic Testing:**
- For forged flanges (if specified)
- Checks for internal defects
- Per ASME Section V
- Expensive but thorough

**Ultrasonic Testing:**
- Alternative to radiography
- Detects internal flaws
- Faster than RT
- Requires skilled operator

**Magnetic Particle Testing:**
- Surface and near-surface defects
- Not applicable to non-magnetic Hastelloy (usually)
- Sometimes specified anyway (won't find defects but proves non-magnetic)

**Liquid Penetrant Testing (PT):**
- Surface defects only
- Economical
- Easy to interpret
- Good for weld inspection

## Industry-Specific Standards

### ASME B31.3: Process Piping

**Design code for chemical and petroleum facilities.**

**References:**
- ASME B16.5 for flange dimensions
- ASTM B564 for material
- Allowable stresses for Hastelloy
- Temperature limits

**Design considerations:**
- Pressure design thickness
- Stress intensification factors
- Flexibility analysis
- Expansion and support

### ASME Section VIII: Pressure Vessels

**When flanges connect to vessels.**

**Requirements:**
- Flange rating adequate for vessel MAWP
- Material compatibility
- Corrosion allowance (typically zero for Hastelloy)
- Documentation and traceability

### API Standards

**American Petroleum Institute:**

**API 6A:**
- Wellhead and Christmas tree equipment
- Higher pressure ratings
- Additional testing requirements
- Hastelloy often specified for sour service

**API 600/602:**
- Valve standards
- Flange connections
- Material requirements

### ANSI/ISA-75.25.01

**Control valve seat leakage classifications.**

**Impacts gasket selection** for control valve flanges.

## Quality Management Standards

### ISO 9001: Quality Management

**Manufacturer certification:**
- Quality system documented
- Consistent processes
- Traceability
- Continuous improvement

**Look for ISO 9001:2015 certified suppliers.**

### ISO/IEC 17025: Testing Laboratories

**For material testing labs:**
- Competence verified
- Equipment calibrated
- Procedures validated
- Results reliable

**Ensure MTRs come from accredited labs.**

### ASME Certification

**ASME stamp for pressure-bearing items:**
- "U" stamp (Section VIII vessels)
- "S" stamp (power boilers)
- Material suppliers often hold ASME certificates

## Documentation Requirements

### Minimum Required Documentation

**Every Hastelloy flange should include:**

1. **Material Test Report (3.1 Cert)**
   - Heat number
   - Chemical composition
   - Mechanical properties

2. **Dimensional Inspection Report**
   - ASME B16.5 compliance
   - Critical dimension verification

3. **Heat Treatment Chart**
   - Time and temperature
   - Cooling rate
   - Verification of proper treatment

4. **Manufacturer's Certification**
   - Compliance with purchase order
   - Statement of conformity

### Enhanced Documentation (Critical Service)

**Additional records for critical applications:**

5. **Traceability Documentation**
   - Melt shop records
   - Forging facility records
   - Complete manufacturing history

6. **Non-Destructive Testing Reports**
   - UT, RT, PT as specified
   - Acceptance criteria
   - Test procedure references

7. **PMI Test Results**
   - Independent verification
   - Documented for each piece

8. **Surface Finish Verification**
   - Ra or RMS values
   - Location-specific measurements

9. **Witness Test Reports**
   - Inspector present during testing
   - Third-party verification
   - Photographic evidence

## Marking Requirements

### ASME B16.5 Marking

**Required marks on flange:**
- Manufacturer's name or trademark
- Material designation (ASTM B564 N10276 or "Hastelloy C276")
- Pressure class (e.g., "300")
- Size (NPS)
- Heat number

**Optional marks:**
- Customer part number
- Purchase order number
- Sequential serial number

### Marking Methods

**Acceptable:**
- Low-stress stamps
- Electrolytic etching
- Laser marking

**Not acceptable:**
- Hard stamps (risk of cracking initiation)
- Paint or ink only (not permanent)

## Special Service Requirements

### Oxygen Service

**Additional cleaning:**
- ASTM G93: Cleanliness for oxygen service
- Packaging requirements
- Documentation of cleaning process

### High-Purity Applications

**Pharmaceutical, semiconductor:**
- Surface finish: 32 Ra or better
- Additional cleaning validation
- Particle count verification
- Bagging and double-bagging

### Nuclear Service

**ASME Section III:**
- Enhanced material traceability
- Additional testing
- Quality assurance program
- N-stamp certification

## Compliance Verification

### How to Verify Compliance

**Review documentation:**
1. Match heat number on flange to MTR
2. Verify chemical composition within specification
3. Check mechanical properties meet requirements
4. Confirm dimensional compliance
5. Verify markings are clear and accurate

**Physical verification:**
1. PMI testing (independent verification)
2. Dimensional inspection (spot-check)
3. Surface finish measurement
4. Visual inspection for damage

**Red flags:**
- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Heat numbers don't match
- Test results out of specification
- Poor quality marks or missing marks
- Price too good to be true
