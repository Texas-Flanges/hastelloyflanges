# Hastelloy Flange Dimensions & Pressure Ratings Guide

Proper specification of **Hastelloy flange** dimensions and pressure ratings is critical for safe, reliable piping systems. This comprehensive guide covers ASME standards, sizing considerations, and selection criteria to ensure you get exactly what your application requires.

## Understanding ASME Flange Standards

### ASME B16.5 (NPS 1/2" to 24")

The primary standard for pipe flanges in smaller sizes. **Hastelloy flanges** manufactured to B16.5 specifications ensure:
- Dimensional interchangeability
- Predictable performance
- Industry-wide compatibility
- Established design practices

### ASME B16.47 (NPS 26" to 60")

For large diameter flanges, B16.47 provides specifications in two series:
- **Series A**: Similar to B16.5 extended dimensions
- **Series B**: More commonly available, slightly different bolt patterns

## Pressure Class Ratings

Pressure class indicates the maximum pressure at specific temperatures. Higher classes = higher pressure capability = larger, heavier flanges.

### Class 150

**Temperature-pressure relationship:**
- 100°F (38°C): 290 psi
- 400°F (204°C): 260 psi
- 600°F (316°C): 180 psi

**Typical applications:**
- Low-pressure chemical transfer
- Water treatment systems
- General industrial piping

**Hastelloy advantage**: Even at Class 150, corrosion resistance provides longevity unmatched by standard materials.

### Class 300

**Temperature-pressure relationship:**
- 100°F: 740 psi
- 400°F: 655 psi
- 600°F: 535 psi

**Typical applications:**
- Medium-pressure chemical processes
- Steam systems
- Refinery piping

**Most common class** for corrosive service applications.

### Class 600

**Temperature-pressure relationship:**
- 100°F: 1480 psi
- 400°F: 1315 psi
- 600°F: 1075 psi

**Typical applications:**
- High-pressure reactors
- Severe service conditions
- Critical process streams

### Class 900

**Temperature-pressure relationship:**
- 100°F: 2220 psi
- 400°F: 1970 psi
- 600°F: 1615 psi

**Typical applications:**
- Very high-pressure systems
- Compressor discharge lines
- High-pressure injection systems

### Class 1500 & 2500

**Extreme pressure applications:**
- Class 1500: Up to 3705 psi @ 100°F
- Class 2500: Up to 6170 psi @ 100°F

**Typical applications:**
- Ultra-high pressure reactors
- Specialized chemical processes
- Research and development systems

## Dimensional Specifications by Size

### Small Bore (NPS 1/2" to 2")

**NPS 1"** **Hastelloy flange**, Class 300, Weld Neck example:
- Outside diameter: 4.88"
- Bolt circle diameter: 3.50"
- Number of bolts: 4
- Bolt size: 5/8"
- Flange thickness: 0.69"
- Hub diameter: 1.69"

**Common applications:**
- Instrument connections
- Sampling lines
- Small chemical feeds

### Medium Bore (NPS 2-1/2" to 12")

**NPS 6" Hastelloy flange**, Class 300, Weld Neck example:
- Outside diameter: 12.50"
- Bolt circle diameter: 10.62"
- Number of bolts: 8
- Bolt size: 7/8"
- Flange thickness: 1.38"
- Hub diameter: 8.19"

**Common applications:**
- Main process lines
- Transfer systems
- Reactor connections

### Large Bore (NPS 14" to 48")

**NPS 24" Hastelloy flange**, Class 300, Weld Neck example:
- Outside diameter: 32.00"
- Bolt circle diameter: 29.50"
- Number of bolts: 20
- Bolt size: 1-3/8"
- Flange thickness: 2.88"
- Hub diameter: 27.44"

**Common applications:**
- Main headers
- Large vessel connections
- High-flow systems

## Bolt Hole Specifications

### Bolt Hole Diameter
Typically bolt diameter + 1/16" for clearance:
- 5/8" bolt: 11/16" hole
- 7/8" bolt: 15/16" hole
- 1-1/4" bolt: 1-5/16" hole

### Bolt Hole Straddling
Flanges can be oriented with:
- Holes straddling centerlines (standard)
- Holes on centerlines (optional)

Specify orientation for proper valve/equipment mounting.

## Facing Types and Dimensions

### Raised Face (RF) - Most Common

**1/16" raised face** (Classes 150 & 300):
- Projects 1/16" above flange face
- Standard for most applications
- Accommodates various gasket types

**1/4" raised face** (Classes 400+):
- Projects 1/4" above flange face
- Higher pressure applications
- Increased gasket compression

### Flat Face (FF)

- No raised surface
- Full face gasket coverage
- Used with:
  - Cast iron equipment
  - Glass-lined vessels
  - Enamel-coated equipment

### Ring Type Joint (RTJ)

Grooves machined for metal ring gaskets:

**Oval grooves**:
- Lower pressure applications
- Easier machining
- More common

**Octagonal grooves**:
- Higher pressure capability
- Better sealing at extreme conditions
- More expensive

## Weight Considerations

**Hastelloy flanges** weigh approximately:
- **Similar** to stainless steel (density ~8.9 g/cm³)
- **Heavier** than aluminum or titanium
- **Lighter** than some steel alloys

**Example weights** (6" Class 300 WN):
- Carbon steel: 46 lbs
- 316 Stainless: 46 lbs
- Hastelloy C276: 48 lbs

Weight matters for:
- Structural support design
- Installation logistics
- Shipping costs

## Hub Dimensions and Importance

The hub (neck) of weld neck flanges provides critical stress distribution.

### Hub Thickness
Gradually transitions from pipe wall to flange thickness:
- Reduces stress concentration
- Improves fatigue life
- Essential for pressure integrity

### Hub Length
Longer hubs provide:
- Better stress distribution
- Improved weld geometry
- Enhanced cycling performance

For **Hastelloy pipe flanges** in critical service, weld neck with proper hub design is almost always preferred.

## Tolerance Requirements

ASME B16.5 specifies tolerances for:

### Flange Face Finish
- **125-250 RMS**: Standard raised face
- **63-125 RMS**: Smoother finishes for special applications
- **32 RMS**: High-purity or sanitary applications

### Dimensional Tolerances
- Flange diameter: ±1/16"
- Bolt circle: ±1/32"
- Bolt holes: +1/16", -0"
- Thickness: ±1/16"

## Temperature Derating

Pressure ratings decrease with temperature. Always check temperature-pressure tables.

**Example: 6" Class 300 Hastelloy flange**
- -20°F to 100°F: 740 psi
- 200°F: 710 psi
- 400°F: 655 psi
- 600°F: 535 psi
- 650°F: 465 psi

**Critical**: Hastelloy's strength retention at elevated temperatures often allows it to outperform other materials that lose strength faster.

## Gasket Seating Stress

Different pressure classes provide different levels of gasket compression:

- **Class 150**: ~5,000 psi seating stress
- **Class 300**: ~9,000 psi seating stress
- **Class 600**: ~14,000 psi seating stress

Higher seating stress generally improves seal integrity but requires:
- Appropriate gasket materials
- Proper bolt torque
- Good surface finish

## Special Considerations for Hastelloy

### Thermal Expansion
Hastelloy's coefficient of thermal expansion:
- 11.3 × 10⁻⁶ /°F (C276)
- Similar to stainless steel
- Account for expansion in piping design

### Cold Service
Unlike some materials, **Hastelloy flanges**:
- Maintain toughness to -196°C
- No ductile-brittle transition
- Ideal for cryogenic applications

### Magnetic Properties
- Non-magnetic in annealed condition
- May show slight magnetism after cold work
- Important for some specialized applications

## Selection Checklist

When specifying **Hastelloy flanges**, verify:

1. **Size (NPS or DN)**
   - Match to pipe outside diameter
   - Consider flow requirements

2. **Pressure Class**
   - Design pressure × safety factor
   - Consider temperature derating

3. **Flange Type**
   - Weld neck (preferred for critical service)
   - Slip-on, socket weld, etc.

4. **Facing**
   - Raised face (most common)
   - Flat face or RTJ as required

5. **Material Grade**
   - C276, C22, B-3, etc.
   - Match to corrosion requirements

6. **Special Requirements**
   - Surface finish
   - Additional testing
   - Documentation needs

## Common Specification Errors

### Mistake 1: Undersizing Pressure Class
Don't forget temperature derating! Many failures occur because flanges were rated at ambient temperature while operating much hotter.

### Mistake 2: Wrong Facing Type
Mixing RF and FF flanges causes leaks. Always verify both mating flanges match.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Hub Requirements
Slip-on flanges cost less but may not handle the stress of critical applications. Don't sacrifice safety for initial cost savings.

### Mistake 4: Inadequate Bolting
Using fewer or smaller bolts than specified compromises seal integrity. Follow ASME requirements exactly.

Proper specification of **Hastelloy flange** dimensions and pressure ratings ensures safe, reliable operation in corrosive environments. Understanding ASME standards, pressure-temperature relationships, and dimensional requirements prevents costly mistakes and enhances system longevity.
