# Hastelloy Flanges: Types, Applications & Specifications

Selecting the right **Hastelloy flanges** for your industrial application requires understanding the various types available, their specific characteristics, and how they align with your operational requirements. This comprehensive guide breaks down everything you need to know.

## Types of Hastelloy Flanges

### Weld Neck Flanges (WN)

**Best for**: High-pressure, high-temperature applications

Weld neck **Hastelloy flanges** feature a long, tapered hub that gradually transitions to the pipe wall thickness. This design provides exceptional structural integrity and stress distribution.

**Key advantages:**
- Superior fatigue resistance
- Excellent for cyclic temperature variations
- Smooth bore transition reduces turbulence
- Preferred for critical service applications

**Common applications:**
- Chemical reactors
- High-pressure steam lines
- Refinery process piping

### Slip-On Flanges (SO)

**Best for**: Lower pressure, non-critical applications

Slip-on flanges slide over the pipe and are then fillet welded at both the top and bottom. They're more economical than weld neck flanges but offer less structural strength.

**Key advantages:**
- Lower initial cost
- Easier alignment during installation
- Suitable for frequent modifications
- Faster installation time

**Common applications:**
- Low-pressure chemical transfer lines
- Cooling water systems
- Non-critical process piping

### Socket Weld Flanges (SW)

**Best for**: Small diameter, high-pressure piping

Socket weld **Hastelloy pipe flanges** are designed for pipes 2 inches and smaller. The pipe inserts into a socket and is then fillet welded externally.

**Key advantages:**
- Excellent for small-bore connections
- High fatigue resistance
- Smooth internal bore
- Reduced fitting-up time

**Common applications:**
- Instrument connections
- Sampling lines
- Small-diameter process piping

### Blind Flanges (BL)

**Best for**: Closing pipe ends and providing access points

Blind flanges are solid disks used to seal the end of piping systems, vessels, or valves. They're essential for pressure testing and providing future access points.

**Key advantages:**
- Complete system isolation
- Easy access for inspection
- Pressure testing capabilities
- Future expansion points

**Common applications:**
- Vessel heads
- Pipeline terminations
- Access manholes

### Threaded Flanges (TH)

**Best for**: Systems requiring frequent disassembly

Threaded flanges have internal threads for pipe connection, eliminating welding requirements. However, they're generally limited to lower-pressure applications.

**Key advantages:**
- No welding required
- Quick assembly/disassembly
- Useful in explosive atmospheres
- Easy maintenance access

**Common applications:**
- Low-pressure systems
- Areas where welding is prohibited
- Temporary installations

### Lap Joint Flanges (LJ)

**Best for**: Systems requiring frequent dismantling and alignment flexibility

Used with stub ends, lap joint flanges allow for easy bolt hole alignment and are ideal for systems requiring regular inspection or cleaning.

**Key advantages:**
- Easy alignment adjustment
- Reduced facing damage
- Lower cost (backing flange in carbon steel)
- Simplified disassembly

**Common applications:**
- Food processing
- Pharmaceutical systems
- Frequent maintenance points

## Hastelloy Alloy Grades for Flanges

### Hastelloy C276

**The industry standard** for chemical processing applications.

**Composition highlights:**
- Nickel: 57%
- Molybdenum: 16%
- Chromium: 16%
- Iron: 5%

**Resistance properties:**
- Outstanding resistance to oxidizing and reducing acids
- Excellent resistance to pitting and crevice corrosion
- Superior chloride stress corrosion cracking resistance
- Temperature range: -196°C to 650°C

**Primary applications:**
- Acetic acid production
- Chlorination systems
- Flue gas desulfurization
- Pesticide manufacturing

### Hastelloy C22

**Enhanced version** with superior oxidizing media resistance.

**Key improvements over C276:**
- Better resistance to oxidizing media
- Enhanced resistance to localized corrosion
- Improved weldability
- Wider application range

**Primary applications:**
- Bleach manufacturing
- Chlorine dioxide production
- Mixed acid pickling
- Waste treatment systems

### Hastelloy B-3

**Specialized grade** for reducing environments.

**Optimized for:**
- Hydrochloric acid at all concentrations
- Sulfuric acid (non-oxidizing)
- Acetic and phosphoric acids
- Temperature stability during welding

**Primary applications:**
- Acid pickling operations
- Chemical manufacturing
- Aluminum chloride processing

### Hastelloy X

**High-temperature grade** for extreme thermal conditions.

**Special characteristics:**
- Excellent high-temperature strength
- Outstanding oxidation resistance
- Good formability and weldability
- Service temperature up to 1200°C

**Primary applications:**
- Gas turbine components
- Industrial furnaces
- Combustion equipment

## Technical Specifications

### Size Standards

**NPS (Nominal Pipe Size):**
- Small bore: 1/2" to 2"
- Medium bore: 2-1/2" to 12"
- Large bore: 14" to 48"
- Custom sizes: Available on request

**DN (Diameter Nominal):**
- DN15 to DN1200
- Metric sizing for international projects

### Pressure Ratings

**ASME B16.5 Classes:**
- Class 150: Up to 290 psi @ 100°F
- Class 300: Up to 740 psi @ 100°F
- Class 600: Up to 1480 psi @ 100°F
- Class 900: Up to 2220 psi @ 100°F
- Class 1500: Up to 3705 psi @ 100°F
- Class 2500: Up to 6170 psi @ 100°F

*Note: Pressure ratings decrease with temperature increases.*

### Facing Types

**Raised Face (RF):**
- Most common
- 1/16" or 1/4" raised surface
- Suitable for most gasket types

**Flat Face (FF):**
- Used with brittle mating surfaces
- Cast iron or glass-lined equipment
- Full gasket contact

**Ring Type Joint (RTJ):**
- High-pressure applications
- Metal-to-metal seal
- Oval or octagonal ring grooves

## Application Guidelines

### Chemical Processing

**Hastelloy flanges** excel in:
- Chlorine and chloride environments
- Mixed acid applications
- Organic acid handling
- Polymer production

### Oil & Gas

Critical for:
- Sour gas service (H2S environments)
- Offshore platforms
- Refinery process units
- Sulfuric acid alkylation

### Pharmaceutical

Essential in:
- Active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) production
- Sterile processing areas
- Clean-in-place (CIP) systems
- High-purity applications

### Power Generation

Used extensively in:
- Flue gas desulfurization (FGD) systems
- Scrubber systems
- Stack gas monitoring
- Emissions control

## Selection Criteria

When choosing **Hastelloy pipe flanges**, consider:

### 1. Operating Conditions
- Maximum/minimum temperature
- Pressure requirements
- Cyclic vs. continuous operation
- Thermal cycling frequency

### 2. Chemical Environment
- Primary corrosive agents
- Concentration levels
- Presence of oxidizers
- pH levels

### 3. Installation Requirements
- Welding constraints
- Space limitations
- Alignment tolerances
- Future maintenance needs

### 4. Budget Considerations
- Initial capital cost
- Expected service life
- Maintenance frequency
- Downtime costs

## Quality Standards and Certifications

Ensure your **Hastelloy flanges** meet:

- **ASME B16.5**: Pipe flanges and flanged fittings
- **ASME B16.47**: Large diameter steel flanges
- **ASTM B564**: Nickel alloy forgings
- **NACE MR0175**: Sulfide stress cracking resistant materials
- **PED 2014/68/EU**: European pressure equipment directive
